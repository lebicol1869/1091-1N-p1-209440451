JULY 6, 2020 BY ERIN & DARA

Overnight Spinach and Cheese Strata

It seems that for a meal to be truly satisfying for me these days, it must contain some form of carbs and cheese. I guess that shouldn’t come as a surprise since 99% of the recipes we post here follow that formula.

It should also come as no surprise then that this Spinach and Cheese Strata is a household favourite for any meal. It was a recipe we originally created for our friends at Canola Eat Well and it’s become a staple ever since. And it’s got bread and cheese. Satisfaction guaranteed.

It’s the perfect vehicle for using up some bread about to go stale. Actually, it works best with a loaf of day or two day old bread as it allows it to soak in that egg mixture even more. We use frozen spinach here, but if you’ve got fresh kicking around you could cook that up with the onion too.

We eat this for dinner just as much as we do for breakfast. Feeding a family? You can easily deconstruct it for a perfect family friendly meal. Leave some of the baguette and cheese plain on the side and scramble up an egg or two if this is a new dish for your kiddos.

While it comes together lickety-split, it does need some time in the fridge to let the bread get all soaked up in that delicious cheesy egg mixture. So make sure to do a little planning and assemble it the day before your planning on digging in.

Having an outdoor brunch party? I couldn’t think of a more delicious dish!

Dara

Barley Bowls with Roasted Tofu and Broccoli

Happy Summer!

Well, happy almost summer. The official first day of summer is June 20th, but that’s just semantics because it sure as heck feels like summer over here. So I’m going with it.

It’s hard to believe we are into the summer season. I think the last time I was out of my house, and I mean really out of my house, running errands like we all used to, I was wearing a winter coat. Yet somehow, three months have flown by, my kids have grown so much and I have grown ever so tired. But it’s summer. The sun is shining, the birds are chirping and the weather is so very warm. So let’s focus on the positive.

Another bright spot today is this Barley Bowl with Tofu and Roasted Broccoli. Such simple ingredients, yet the result is delicious. You’ve also probably got everything you need already lurking in your pantry or fridge. Barley is a pantry staple here for many reasons. It’s hearty and satisfying – I’m a sucker for that chewiness. It’s also such a versatile grain. You can cook it up in soup, in a salad, use it instead of rice in risotto or go the sweet route and enjoy it for breakfast like you would oatmeal. And in this dish? It’s just delicious.

The first time I’m able to invite people over for dinner, I’m definitely cooking this for them. Why? It can be served up in so many different ways.
